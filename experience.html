Lynn Kocal's Experience

I started out in the publishing business over 30 years ago, setting type on a Linotype for body type, and a Ludlow that made headlines from hot lead. I stood at a Miehle press and fed sheets of paper into the platen press until there were about 1,000 of each 2 sided sheet that were distributed in our small city of Lanark, Illinois.

Over the years my husband and I added employees, computers and equipment to the newspaper and had a circulation of about 15,000.

I built the paper's first website in 1997 and, even though HTML wasn't very sophisticated and there was no CSS then, that's where the love affair began.

There are many people who helped me learn along the way and I am grateful to them all. One is my son-in-law, a senior developer who built our second iteration of a website and showed me how to edit it as well as inspiring me to learn all I could on my own.
